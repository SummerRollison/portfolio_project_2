About Me

Coming from a family of writers and crafters, it was only natural that I inherited the creativity bug. As a kid, it appeared through an interest in making anything I could out of duct tape. Growing up, I went through various hobbies including cake decorating and a very brief jewelry making stage. But, when I landed in the yearbook classroom in high school it was more than just a phase or hobby. InDesign is where I first fell in love with digital design, and though I do frequently flirt with Illustrator and Photoshop, layout is the sweet spot for me. Web design has always felt like a natural extension of my passion for layout.

Outside of digital, I also got creative in the self identity department with my name. At age 20, while living as a nanny in Seattle, I decided to change my name solely because I had never been a fan of my given one. The Seattle rain made me crave sunshine, so I chose Summer.

My husband and I unexpectedly got the chance to relocate and now we’re moving back to our home state, exactly where we always wanted to be. In doing so, I’m leaving a relatively new job that has provided me with the opportunity to pursue my passions. I’m looking for a similarly energized team to collaborate and work alongside. I thrive in a team environment where we push ourselves to work harder and feed off of each others ideas. Progression, collaboration, and creativity drive my desire to find a new team in my old home.
